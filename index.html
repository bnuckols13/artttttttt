⊙ Understanding Our Inner System

Internal Family Systems (IFS) therapy teaches that our psyche is naturally multiple - we have many different "parts" or sub-personalities, each with its own perspective, feelings, and role. Rather than seeing these parts as problems, IFS invites us to understand them as protective aspects of ourselves, each trying to help in its own way.

◇ Self / Core

The calm, compassionate center that can hold all parts. Characterized by the 8 C's: Curiosity, Clarity, Compassion, Connectedness, Confidence, Courage, Creativity, Calm.

▽ Exiles

Vulnerable parts carrying painful emotions from the past. Often young, hurt, scared, or ashamed. They hold our wounds and unmet needs.

△ Managers

Protective parts that try to control situations to prevent pain. They plan, criticize, strive for perfection, people-please, intellectualize.

⬡ Firefighters

Reactive parts that emerge when exiles break through. They distract, numb, escape through substances, binge behaviors, dissociation, or rage.

⊙ Key IFS Concepts for Today

◈ Positive Intent ▼

Every part, no matter how destructive its behavior, has a positive intention. The harsh inner critic is trying to protect you from failure. The part that numbs with substances is trying to protect you from overwhelming pain. Understanding the positive intent helps us approach parts with curiosity rather than judgment.

◈ Polarization ▼

Parts can be in conflict - one part wants to work hard, another wants to rest. One part wants connection, another wants safety through isolation. These polarizations are normal. The goal isn't to eliminate one side, but to help both parts feel heard and find a balanced way forward.

◈ Unblending ▼

When we're "blended" with a part, we feel completely taken over by it - "I AM anxious" rather than "I NOTICE an anxious part." Unblending means creating space to observe the part from Self. This allows curiosity and compassion to emerge. Creative expression naturally supports unblending by externalizing inner experience.

◈ The 6 F's of IFS Work ▼

While formal IFS therapy follows these steps systematically, today's creative exploration touches on them organically:

  • Find: Notice and locate a part
  • Focus: Give it attention and space
  • Flesh out: Learn about it through creative expression
  • Feel toward: Notice how you relate to this part
  • beFriend: Build relationship and trust
  • Fears: Understand what this part is protecting you from

⊙ Meeting Your Parts Today

Important Reminder: This is an introduction to parts work, not therapy. If working with a specific part brings up intense emotions, that's a sign this part might need more support than a group setting can provide. Consider working with an IFS-trained therapist for deeper healing.

As you move through the creative stations today, you might choose to:

  • Focus on one part deeply
  • Explore multiple parts across different mediums
  • Create a dialogue between polarized parts
  • Give voice to an exile that rarely gets heard
  • Thank a protector for its hard work
  • Connect with Self and its qualities

⊙ Seven Creative Pathways

Choose 2-3 stations that call to you. There's no right order. Let your parts guide you to the mediums that want expression today. Remember: you can always pass on any prompt that doesn't feel right.

Station Structure: Spend 15-20 minutes at each station you choose. Take breaks as needed. Move at your own pace.
1

Visual Arts Station

Draw, paint, or collage your parts as characters, symbols, or abstract forms

  • Create a "parts portrait" - draw different parts as distinct characters or figures in one image
  • Give each part a color, shape, or symbol that represents its energy
  • Draw your internal landscape - where do your parts live? Mountains, caves, gardens, rooms?
  • Create a comic strip showing a conversation between two polarized parts
  • Paint your Self (core essence) at the center with parts arranged around it
  • Use abstract art to show how a part FEELS rather than what it looks like
Materials available: Paper, markers, colored pencils, watercolors, magazines for collage, pastels
2

Movement & Embodiment

Explore how different parts move, hold tension, or express themselves through the body

  • Move as a manager part - how does it hold the body? Sharp? Tense? Controlled?
  • Embody a firefighter part - quick movements, escape, distraction
  • Give gentle movement to an exile - small, protective, vulnerable gestures
  • Create a movement sequence showing transition from blended to unblended state
  • Dance a dialogue between two parts - let them "speak" through gesture
  • Find the movement of Self - grounded, flowing, centered
Note: This can be gentle and small. You don't need to move your whole body. Hand gestures, facial expressions, or subtle shifts in posture all count.
3

Music & Sound Station

Assign soundscapes, songs, or rhythms to different parts

  • Create a playlist for different parts - what songs represent them?
  • Use instruments to give each part a sound (drum, bells, shakers, voice)
  • What's the tempo of your manager parts? Your exiles? Your firefighters?
  • Compose a simple melody or rhythm that represents Self energy
  • Notice what music helps parts feel heard or helps them relax
  • Create a "parts symphony" - how would they sound playing together?
Materials available: Simple percussion instruments, access to music streaming for playlist creation, voice recording option
4

Writing & Dialogue

Write letters, dialogues, or narratives from and to your parts

  • Write a dialogue between a protective part and the exile it's protecting
  • Let a part write a letter to you from its perspective: "What you don't know about me is..."
  • Journal as different parts - give each one a voice
  • Write a "parts meeting" - what would they say to each other?
  • Create a poem from the voice of Self, speaking to all parts
  • Write the origin story of a part: "I was born when..."
Tip: Try writing with your non-dominant hand when dialoguing with younger, exile parts. It can help access different states.
5

Drama & Role-Play

Act out, embody, or give voice to different parts through dramatic expression

  • Create a short scene between two parts having a conversation
  • Use objects or puppets to represent different parts and have them interact
  • Practice saying out loud what each part's core message is
  • Physically place yourself in different locations in the room as different parts
  • Use masks or simple costume pieces to embody parts briefly
  • Record yourself speaking as different parts - notice tone, pace, volume changes
Privacy option: This can be done quietly, just for yourself. You don't need to perform for others.
6

Fashion & Symbolic Adornment

Create wearable representations of parts or Self through fabric, jewelry, or symbolic items

  • Design a bracelet or charm for a specific part
  • Choose colors and textures that represent different parts
  • Create a "Self" token to carry as a reminder of your centered core
  • Make simple masks that represent protective vs. vulnerable parts
  • Design a "parts wardrobe" - what would each part wear?
  • Create a small talisman that represents integration of parts
Materials available: Beads, string, fabric scraps, ribbons, simple clay, decorative items
7

Open Intuitive Exploration

Follow your instinct - combine mediums or create in whatever way calls to you

  • Build a three-dimensional "parts council" using found objects
  • Create a parts map showing relationships, distances, conflicts
  • Make a meditation guide for connecting with Self
  • Design a "parts board game" showing the journey toward integration
  • Create whatever your parts are asking to create right now
  • Combine multiple mediums to tell the story of one part's journey
This is your space: There are no wrong ways to explore. Trust your creative instincts.

⊙ Closing Ritual

Guided Closing (5 minutes)

Take a comfortable position and close your eyes if that feels safe.
Take three deep breaths.

Imagine gathering all the parts you met today into a circle.
From your Self - that calm, compassionate center - thank each part for showing up.
Thank the managers for working so hard to keep you safe.
Thank the firefighters for stepping in when things felt overwhelming.
Thank the exiles for their courage in being seen.
Thank your Self for creating space to hold them all.

Know that these parts are always with you.
You can return to this creative conversation with them anytime.
They don't need to be fixed or changed - just witnessed, understood, and held with compassion.

When you're ready, take a final deep breath and open your eyes.
Welcome back.
